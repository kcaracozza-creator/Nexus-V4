| Trading card market growth | 8.5% CAGR through 2032 | Grand View Research |

### Why 1B Is Justified
- Only 20M cards get graded today out of billions produced each year. That's a <0.5% grading rate.
- Why? Because grading costs $25-$150 and takes weeks. At those economics, only high-value cards are worth grading.
- NEXUS at $3-$12 with 48-hour turnaround removes every barrier. A $5 card is now worth grading at $3.
- At $3/grade, even bulk commons make sense for collectors wanting certified collections.
- If 10-20% of cards produced annually get graded (vs. today's <0.5%), that's 500M-2B cards.
- 1B is the midpoint — a 50x increase from today's 20M, achieved by making grading 5-50x cheaper and 10x faster.
- **The comparison**: PSA grades 15M/yr at $25+ average. Drop the price 80% and the volume doesn't just 5x — it 50x, because you've unlocked the entire long tail of cards that were never economically viable to grade.
- **Verdict: The math is sound. Price elasticity in grading is extreme. PSA proved the demand at $25 — NEXUS unlocks the ocean of demand at $3-$5.**
